Workflow 3: Live Info Session Registration

The Resilience Project — CRM Workflow Documentation for Dynamics 365 Migration • April 2026

Summary: A school registers for an upcoming live information session (webinar). The CRM must capture the registration, create or update a sales opportunity, and trigger a 6-touch automated communications sequence spanning from registration through to 2 weeks after the event.

1. Trigger

A school staff member submits the "Info Session Registration" form on the TRP website, selecting a specific upcoming session date.

The form supports pre-filling — if the contact arrives via a personalised link (e.g. from a previous email), their details are automatically populated to reduce friction.

3. Business Process

3.1 Identify or create the contact and organisation

  • Same rules as other workflows: link to existing org or create new, one active contact per email, assign owner by state.

3.2 Branch: New school vs. Existing partnership

New schools (no dedicated SPM) receive the full automated journey: deal creation, event registration, and the 6-touch comms sequence. Existing schools (with a dedicated SPM) are handled differently — the SPM is simply notified and manages follow-up personally.

3.3 New school path (most registrations)

  1. Create or update the sales opportunity (Deal)
    • Deal stage: "In Campaign" with "Hot" rating — the school is actively engaging
    • Expected close date: day after the event
    • Track the date of the school's first info session registration on the deal
  2. Suppress previous nurture comms
    • If this school previously entered the "Request for Info" nurture sequence (Workflow 2), those automated emails must stop. The school has progressed beyond that stage.
  3. Register for the info session
    • The contact is registered for the selected event
    • This triggers the automated communications sequence (see Section 4)

3.4 Existing school path

  • The dedicated SPM is notified that their school has expressed interest in a live info session.
  • No event registration or automated comms — the SPM manages the relationship directly.

4. Communications Sequence

New school registrations trigger a 6-touch automated communications sequence anchored to the event date:

1. Registration Confirmation

Sent immediately

Thanks the registrant, confirms event details (date, time, video link), sets expectations for the session.

2. One Week Before

7 days before event

Reminder with session details and video link. Builds anticipation and reduces no-shows.

3. Day Before

1 day before event

Final reminder with video link and any last-minute details.

4. Recording Access

After the event

Link to the session recording — for attendees to revisit and for those who missed the live session.

5. One Week Post-Event

7 days after event

Follow-up — checking if they have questions or are ready to take the next step.

Condition: Only sent if the school hasn't already progressed in the sales pipeline.

6. Two Weeks Post-Event

14 days after event

Final automated follow-up. If the school still hasn't progressed, the deal interest level is downgraded to "Cold".

Condition: Only sent if the school hasn't already progressed in the sales pipeline.

Business requirement: Post-event follow-ups (emails 5 and 6) must automatically stop if the salesperson has already advanced the deal. Only schools that haven't engaged further should receive the automated nudges. All emails should come from (or reply-to) the assigned salesperson, so responses go to the right person.

6. Deal Stage Progression

The info session registration is part of a broader sales pipeline. Here's how the deal progresses across workflows:

StageWhat triggers itMeaning
NewGeneral enquiry or info request (Workflow 1 or 2)School has expressed initial interest
In CampaignInfo session registration (this workflow)School is actively engaging with content
Cold (rating)2 weeks post-event with no further engagementInterest appears to have faded

7. Key Business Rules

  1. No duplicate contacts: One active contact per email address.
  2. Existing partnerships bypass automation: Schools with a dedicated SPM are not registered for events or sent automated emails. The SPM is notified and manages follow-up personally.
  3. Nurture suppression: Registering for an info session must stop any "request for info" nurture emails the school was previously receiving.
  4. Conditional post-event comms: Follow-up emails only send if the deal hasn't been manually advanced by the salesperson.
  5. Automatic cold rating: If a school hasn't engaged 2 weeks post-event, the deal is downgraded to "Cold" to reflect diminishing interest.
  6. First session tracking: The deal records which info session the school first registered for, giving the sales team visibility into the engagement timeline.
  7. Form pre-fill: Contacts arriving via personalised links should have their details pre-populated to reduce form friction.
  8. Personalised sender: All automated emails must come from (or reply-to) the assigned salesperson, so responses go to the right person.
