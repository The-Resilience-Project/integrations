Workflow 1: General Enquiry

The Resilience Project — CRM Workflow Documentation for Dynamics 365 Migration • April 2026

Summary: A school, workplace, or early years service submits a general enquiry via our website. The CRM must capture the contact and organisation, create a sales opportunity, assign ownership to the right staff member, log the enquiry, and send a confirmation email.

1. Trigger

A prospective customer submits an enquiry form on the TRP website. The form is used by three service types: School Workplace Early Years.

3. Business Process

3.1 Identify or create the contact and organisation

  • If the organisation already exists in the CRM, link the contact to it.
  • If it's a new organisation, create it and link the contact.
  • Business rule: There must only be one active contact per email address. If the same person submits multiple forms, only the most recent submission should be the active contact record.

3.2 Track engagement history

  • The organisation should track which forms/campaigns any of its contacts have engaged with over time.
  • The contact should track which forms they've personally completed.

3.3 Assign ownership

The contact, organisation, and enquiry must be assigned to the correct staff member. The rules differ by service type:

Key concept — New vs. Existing relationships: Some organisations already have a dedicated relationship manager. These must stay with their existing manager. Organisations that don't yet have a dedicated manager are routed based on state and service type.

Why no deal for existing schools? If a school already has a dedicated SPM, they're an existing partner. Creating a new deal would duplicate the existing relationship. The enquiry record alone is sufficient to notify the SPM.

3.5 Log the enquiry

  • An enquiry record is created capturing the message, service type, and linked to the contact.
  • The enquiry is assigned to the appropriate staff member (same routing rules as above).

3.6 Send confirmation email

  • An automated acknowledgement email is sent to the person who submitted the enquiry.

4. After the Initial Response

Once the assigned staff member (Laura or Brendan) has responded to the enquiry, the deal progresses based on the school's size:

This section applies to schools only. The post-response lifecycle for Workplace and Early Years follows a different process not covered here.

4.1 Large schools (500+ students) → Proactive Sales Pipeline

  • The deal moves into a proactive nurture pipeline managed by the sales team.
  • The salesperson actively works the relationship — calls, emails, follow-ups — to convert the school into a partner.
  • This is a manual, high-touch process because larger schools represent significant partnership value.

4.2 Smaller schools (under 500 students) → Automated Nurture

  • The deal stage moves to "In Campaign".
  • The school enters an automated nurture sequence with 2+ timed communications designed to encourage them to book a live info session.
  • The goal of these emails is to get the school to take a call-to-action: register for a live info session (see Workflow 3).
TimingCommunicationGoal
After initial responseFirst nurture emailEncourage booking a live info session
Follow-up intervalSecond nurture email (and potentially more)Re-engage if no action taken

4.3 If the school doesn't take action

  • If the school doesn't respond to the automated nurture comms (i.e. doesn't book a live info session or otherwise engage), the deal is moved to "Dormant".
  • This signals that the lead has gone cold and is no longer being actively pursued.
Business requirement: Automated nurture emails must stop if the school takes action (e.g. books an info session, responds to the salesperson, or progresses in the pipeline). Only schools that remain unresponsive should receive further nudges before being marked Dormant.

7. CRM Data Model

EntityPurpose
ContactThe person making the enquiry. One active contact per email.
Organisation (Account)The school, workplace, or early years service. Tracks engagement history and owner.
Deal (Opportunity)The sales opportunity. Linked to contact and organisation.
EnquiryThe enquiry message itself. Linked to contact. Used to notify the assigned staff member.

8. Key Business Rules

  1. No duplicate contacts: Only one active contact per email address at any time.
  2. Preserve existing relationships: If an organisation already has a dedicated manager, never reassign it.
  3. No duplicate deals for existing partnerships: Schools with a dedicated SPM don't get new deals — the enquiry alone notifies the SPM.
  4. Engagement tracking: Both the organisation and contact must maintain a history of which forms/campaigns they've engaged with.
  5. State-based routing: NSW and QLD school enquiries route to Brendan; all other states route to Laura. Workplace always routes to Laura. Early Years always routes to Brendan.
  6. Size-based post-response path: After the initial response, schools with 500+ students enter proactive sales nurture; under 500 enter automated campaign nurture.
  7. Automated nurture goal: The purpose of the automated comms for smaller schools is to get them to book a live info session (Workflow 3).
  8. Dormant on no response: If automated nurture comms don't result in engagement, the deal moves to Dormant — no further automated outreach.
  9. Stop comms on action: Automated nurture emails must stop immediately if the school takes any action (books a session, replies, etc.).
